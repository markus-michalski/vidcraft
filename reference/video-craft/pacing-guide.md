# Video Pacing Guide

## Words Per Minute by Content Type

| Content Type | WPM | Rationale |
|-------------|-----|-----------|
| Tutorial (step-by-step) | 120-130 | Viewer needs time to follow along |
| Installation Guide | 130-140 | Clear, sequential, moderate pace |
| Explainer | 140-150 | Engaging, needs to hold attention |
| Product Demo | 140-160 | Energetic, benefit-focused |
| Marketing Spot | 150-170 | Fast, punchy, attention-grabbing |
| Training | 130-140 | Structured, needs comprehension |
| FAQ | 140-150 | Direct, efficient |

## Scene Duration Guidelines

| Scene Type | Min | Max | Sweet Spot |
|-----------|-----|-----|------------|
| Hook/Intro | 5s | 15s | 8-10s |
| Individual Step | 20s | 90s | 30-45s |
| Feature Demo | 20s | 60s | 30-40s |
| Explanation | 15s | 60s | 20-30s |
| Recap/Summary | 10s | 30s | 15-20s |
| CTA | 5s | 15s | 8-10s |

## Viewer Attention Curve

```
100% ┤ ██
 90% ┤ ████
 80% ┤ ██████
 70% ┤ █████████
 60% ┤ █████████████
 50% ┤ ████████████████████
     └──────────────────────────
      0s   30s  1m   2m   5m   10m
```

- First 10 seconds: CRITICAL — hook or lose the viewer
- 30-60 seconds: Establish value proposition
- 2-5 minutes: Core content — maintain with visual variety
- 5+ minutes: Viewer fatigue — use section breaks, summaries

## Pacing Rhythm

Good pacing alternates between:
- **Information** (narration with visuals) — 20-30s
- **Breathing room** (transition, visual pause) — 2-5s
- **Emphasis** (key point, on-screen text highlight) — 5-10s

## Rules of Thumb

1. Change visual every 15-30 seconds (scene, angle, or overlay)
2. No single narration block longer than 45 seconds
3. Include a "breather" every 2-3 minutes in long videos
4. Front-load the most important information
5. End 10-15% shorter than the viewer's expected tolerance
