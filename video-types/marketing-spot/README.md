# Video Type: Marketing Spot

## Overview

Short, emotionally driven promotional videos designed to capture attention and drive action. Marketing spots use the AIDA framework to move viewers from awareness to conversion in under 60 seconds.

## Characteristics

| **Aspect** | **Value** |
|------------|-----------|
| **Duration** | 15-60 seconds |
| **Pacing** | Fast, emotional, high-energy |
| **Tone** | Confident, aspirational, persuasive |
| **WPM** | 150-170 (punchy, rhythmic delivery) |
| **Scene Changes** | Every 5-15 seconds |
| **Primary Visual** | Avatar + Motion Graphics + Product Shots |

## Structure Template

1. **Attention** (3-10s) — Bold hook: pain point, shocking stat, or provocative question
2. **Interest** (5-15s) — Expand the problem, make it relatable
3. **Desire** (10-25s) — Present the solution, show benefits (not features)
4. **Action** (5-10s) — Clear, single CTA with urgency

## Script Conventions

- Lead with emotion, not logic
- Focus on benefits: "Save 3 hours per week" not "Automated scheduling feature"
- Use power words: "transform", "unlock", "instantly", "proven"
- One core message per spot — never dilute with secondary offers
- CTA must be specific and actionable: "Start your free trial today"
- Max 15 words per sentence for punchy delivery
- Rhetorical questions work well as openers

## Visual Direction

- **Primary:** Avatar for emotional delivery, switch to product/motion graphics for proof
- **Hooks:** Full-screen text or dramatic avatar close-up for opening
- **Transitions:** Fast cuts, zoom transitions, kinetic typography
- **On-Screen Text:** Key stats, benefit statements, CTA with URL/button
- **Color/Mood:** High contrast, brand colors, energetic motion

## Pacing Rules

- Max 6 words of on-screen text at once
- No scene longer than 15 seconds
- First 3 seconds must hook — no logos, no intros
- Build tempo: slower at Interest, fastest at Desire, pause before CTA
- Total word count: 40-170 words (scales with duration)

## Anti-Patterns

- Starting with a logo or company introduction
- Listing features instead of benefits
- Multiple CTAs competing for attention
- Generic claims without specifics ("best in class", "world-leading")
- Slow, corporate-style pacing that loses mobile viewers
- Forgetting to create urgency at the CTA

## Example Storyboard

```
Scene 1 (3s): Text overlay — "Still wasting 5 hours a week on manual updates?"
Scene 2 (8s): Avatar — "What if you could get those hours back — starting today?"
Scene 3 (12s): Product shots + motion graphics — Show dashboard, automation in action, time saved counter
Scene 4 (7s): Avatar — "Join 2,000+ shop owners who automated their workflow. Start free at example.com"
```
