# Video Type: Testimonial

## Overview

Authentic customer success stories that build trust through real experiences. Testimonials follow a narrative arc from problem to result, using specific details and genuine language to feel credible rather than scripted. They serve as social proof for prospects in the decision-making phase.

## Characteristics

| **Aspect** | **Value** |
|------------|-----------|
| **Duration** | 60-120 seconds |
| **Pacing** | Authentic, conversational, unhurried |
| **Tone** | Genuine, relatable, enthusiastic but not over-the-top |
| **WPM** | 130-140 (natural speaking pace) |
| **Scene Changes** | Every 15-25 seconds |
| **Primary Visual** | Avatar (as customer) + Product Shots + Results/Data |

## Structure Template

1. **Problem** (10-20s) — Describe the challenge before the solution
2. **Solution** (15-30s) — How the product/service addressed it
3. **Result** (20-40s) — Specific, measurable outcomes
4. **Recommendation** (10-20s) — Who this is for and why they'd recommend it

## Script Conventions

- Write in first person as the customer: "I was struggling with...", "We switched to..."
- Use specific numbers and details: "cut our processing time from 4 hours to 20 minutes"
- Avoid superlatives without evidence: not "the best tool ever" but "saved us 12 hours per week"
- Include the "before" state to create contrast
- Name the specific features or aspects that made the difference
- Keep language conversational — contractions, natural phrasing
- Max 20 words per sentence to maintain natural flow

## Visual Direction

- **Primary:** Avatar as the customer, speaking directly to camera
- **Problem Phase:** Subtle visual cues (muted colors, frustrated expressions)
- **Solution Phase:** Show the product in action, highlight key features
- **Result Phase:** Data visualizations, before/after comparisons, metrics on screen
- **Transitions:** Soft cuts, natural feel — avoid flashy transitions
- **On-Screen Text:** Key metrics, company name/role of the speaker, quote highlights

## Pacing Rules

- Max 8 words of on-screen text at once
- No scene longer than 25 seconds
- Problem section should feel relatable, not overly dramatic
- Results should include at least one specific metric or timeframe
- Allow brief pauses for emphasis after key statements
- Total word count: 130-280 words (scales with duration)

## Anti-Patterns

- Generic praise without specifics ("It's amazing!", "Love it!")
- Sounding scripted or corporate — must feel natural
- Skipping the problem phase — no contrast means no impact
- Unrealistic claims that damage credibility
- Missing concrete results or measurable outcomes
- Overly polished delivery that feels like an ad, not a testimonial

## Example Storyboard

```
Scene 1 (15s): Avatar — "Before switching, we spent every Friday afternoon manually updating our product catalog. Four hours, every week, just copy-pasting data."
Scene 2 (20s): Avatar + Product screenshots — "The import module changed that completely. We set up the CSV mapping once, and now the catalog updates run automatically overnight."
Scene 3 (25s): Avatar + Data overlay — "We went from 4 hours of manual work to zero. And our error rate dropped from about 5% to under 0.1%. In the first month alone, we saved over 16 hours."
Scene 4 (15s): Avatar — "If you're running a shop with more than 500 products, this is a no-brainer. I'd recommend it to any shop owner who values their Friday afternoons."
```
